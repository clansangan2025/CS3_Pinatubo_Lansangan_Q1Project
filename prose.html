Pink Steam

This is a an essay collection/memoir that is all over the place in a good way. There's one part where our protagonist morphs into a reptilian monster, and another where she shoplifts and fails miserably. It's really funny and it gets graphic sometimes, but it's such a rewarding read.

Annihilation

This book starts out slow, progresses slow, and ends... slow. You must be wondering, "well, what's so pyrotechnic about this then?" And you're right - it does drag a bit. But this book wants to disturb you, and if it wants to take its time, it will. We are placed gently into the world of the Area X as a calculating biologist amongst a team of investigators. In the book, we encounter the deterioration of the human, where nature regains claim of what it once owned.

The Circle

This book is a critique on how companies turn into monopolies and how little privacy everyone ever really has in the modern age. Our protagonist Mae lands a job in a prestigious tech-company and gets into all sorts of situations that exposes the criminal doings of The Circle. I really like this book not only because it's relevant, but also because it's written where you're expecting something big coming up but then it doesn't happen so you let your guard down a bit and when the issue is revealed, the impact is so hard it's dizzying... in a good way, of course.
